# DASHBOARD
**Swachh Sarvekshan Performance Dashboard (2016-2023)**

***Project Overview***

This data analytics project provides a longitudinal analysis of the Swachh Sarvekshan, the annual cleanliness survey conducted across Indian cities. The dashboard serves as a strategic tool for visualizing the progress of the Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM), comparing city performance, and evaluating the impact of citizen feedback over an eight-year period.

***Key Features & Visualizations***

The dashboard is divided into several analytical modules to provide a 360-degree view of the data:

1) Geospatial Distribution: A map visualization identifying the concentration of surveyed cities across the Indian subcontinent.

2) Performance Metrics (KPIS):

    2a) Citizen Score: Highlighted at 20K, showing the aggregate engagement of the public.

    2b) Goal Tracking: A comparative analysis showing a massive increase in total scores
        between 2017 and 2022 (+47,281.92% growth).

3) Trend Analysis:

   3a) Score Evolution: Area and line charts showing fluctuations in performance by State and
    Year.
   3b) Cumulative Growth: A gauge chart tracking the "Sum of Citizen Feedback & Swachhata App"
    interactions, currently at 244K.

4) City Rankings & Comparison:

   4a) Top Performers (2023): Pie chart breakdown featuring leading cities like Indore, Surat,
    and Navi Mumbai.

   4b) Historical Breakdown: A stacked bar chart comparing city scores specifically for the
    years 2016, 2017, and 2018.

5) Treemap Analytics: Visual representation of the "Total Score" by city name for rapid identification of high-performing municipalities.

***Technical Stack***

1) Tool: Power BI / Microsoft Business Intelligence

2) Data Sources: Swachh Sarvekshan Annual Reports (2016-2023)

3) Visual Elements: Treemaps, Gauge Charts, Stacked Bar Charts, Pie Charts, and Map APIs.

***Key Insights***

1) Massive Scale-Up: The survey methodology and scoring criteria have expanded significantly, as evidenced by the "Total Score" goal tracking metric.

2) Citizen Centricity: A heavy emphasis on the "Swachhata App" data suggests that citizen feedback is a primary driver in modern city rankings.

3) Regional Leadership: The data consistently highlights competitive performance among major urban hubs while tracking the entry of newer municipalities into the survey framework.
